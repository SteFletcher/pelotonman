Detailed specifications for the simulation & game-loop subsystems

1. RNG Subsystem

Source: src/engine/rng.js. A mulberry32 PRNG with a serializable 32-bit state.

APIBehaviour

The test "identical seeds replay the identical race" deep-equals two simulateRace results with the same seed. Authored rosters consume no RNG, so a fresh career is reproducible from the manager name + team + seed alone.

2. Race Simulation Engine

Source: src/engine/race.js. The most complex subsystem.

2.1 Public API

class RaceSim {
  constructor(teams, profile, options)  // {seed?, gtStage?, timeline?, wind?, autoOrders?}
  playSegment()                         // → Event[] for that segment
  setOrders(teamKey, { chase?, control?, sendUpRoad?, leadout?, attack? })
  setRacePlan(teamKey, { plan?, aggression? })
  setRole(teamKey, riderId, newRole)    // mid-race role reassignment (≤1 LDR swap)
  simulateToEnd()
  finish()                              // → RaceResult (see data model)
}
simulateRace(teams, profile, options)   // convenience: one-shot → RaceResult

2.2 Setup Normalisation

The engine accepts three input shapes, normalised by normalizeSetup():

  1. Full squad (>8 riders): selectRaceTeam auto-picks the 8 + reserves.
  2. Explicit 8 ({name, riders:[8]}): used as-is, role = natural type.
  3. Prepared lineup ({starters:[{rider,role}], reserves, plan, aggression}): the richest form, used by the live UI.

validateSetup() then enforces ≤2 LDR, valid roles, and attribute ranges — throwing descriptive errors before any segment is played.

2.3 The Per-Segment Loop

playSegment():
  km += segmentKm          (5–10 km; 1 km in the final 5)
  for each team: #aiOrdersPolicy ; #aiPlanPolicy
  resolve peloton pace from controlling teams' orders & road profile
    (climb sector flattens pace; descent quickens; headwind taxes breaks)
  breakaway resolution:
      if no break & attack orders present → maybe form a break (riders sent up road)
      if break exists → gap evolves by (breakPace − chasePace) × sector
      if gap < 0 → break caught, reabsorbed into peloton
  domestique work: each pulling rider burns condition, earns pull credit → ratings
  crashes & mechanicals (0.16% per rider/segment, weighted by injuryProne & density):
      dropped rider loses time; serious → abandon (DNF) for the stage/race
  climb sector (if any): re-group by climbing strength; gaps open at summit; KOM points
  intermediate sprint (if any): points to first across; minor time bonus on GT stages
  finish sector: resolve sprint (SPR-led, leadout-modulated) or summit finish (CLM/LDR)
      → assign placings, time gaps, sprint points, time bonuses
  condition decay for pullers/chasers (0.20/km hard, 0.08/km soft)
  push timeline snapshot
  if km ≥ distanceKm: finish()

2.4 Strength Model

Per-rider effective strength is profile-aware:

#eff(rider, sector) = baseAttr(rider, sector)
                    × slotPenalty(rider, role)      // 1.0 natural, 0.8 non-leader in LDR, 0.75 severe
                    × sharpness                     // morale + form + consistency noise
                    × (0.7 + 0.3 × condition/100)
                    × infraBoost(team)              // 1.0 + 0.004 × (infraLevel − 1)

baseAttr by sector:
  flat finish  → power
  climb sector → climb
  ITT          → tt  (= 0.6·power + 0.4·consistency)
  sprint 250m  → power × (1 + 0.15·leadoutBonus)

Team contributions are pooled: a sprint train's leadout bonus is the sum of fresh SPR/ROU riders' power ahead of the sprinter, with diminishing returns, so a full Alpecin–Deceuninck train genuinely delivers Jasper Philipsen faster than a lone sprinter. Headcount on a climb helps a leader's defence but cannot substitute for low climb.

2.6 AI Behaviour

  • Plan policy: AI picks a profile-appropriate default plan (mountain → GC Defence/Climbing Block; flat → Sprint Train; hilly → All-Round/Breakaway). A team with a GC contender in the top 5 defends; a team with no stake sends a rider up the road.
  • Orders policy: when a dangerous break forms and an AI team's leader is behind, it contributes to the chase proportional to its stake. A leading team sets a steady control pace to discourage further breaks.
  • Leadout policy: in the final 5 km of a flat stage, an AI team with a sprinter organises its train and burns its rouleurs in sequence.
  • Forced abandon on serious crash: AI teams accept the loss; user teams are left in trouble to force a managerial reaction (the auto-pause).

2.7 Time Trial & Prologue

When kind:'itt', the race simulates as a staggered individual effort: each rider's time = distanceKm / speed(tt, condition) with small variance from consistency. No peloton, no breaks, no orders. Team time trials (TTT) take the median of the 4th-best rider's time across the 8. ITTs are the one place a rider like Filippo Ganna or Remco Evenepoel can take minutes.

3.2 Standings (computeStandings)

Accumulates points, wins, podiums, and race count per team, then sorts on points → wins → podiums → name. The table is the season-long "league" view.

4. Grand Tour Subsystem (gt.js)

Each Grand Tour is a sequenced set of 21 stages run over its calendar window (3 weeks, with 2 rest days where the engine advances without a stage). createGrandTour seeds the stage list with a realistic profile mix.

Stage mix (21)CountNotes
Flat / sprint stages~9Sprint train territory; minor time bonification at the line.
Hilly / transitional~4Breakaway-prone; late climbs thin the bunch.
Mountain (summit finish)~4GC battle; Cat-1/HC climbs; big time gaps.
Individual time trial~2One short prologue, one long ITT.
Rest days2Engine advances; condition partially recovers.

advanceStage(gt, result) appends the stage result, recomputes GC (Σ time + bonuses − bonifications), and updates the four jerseys: GC (lowest total time), Points (Σ sprint points), KOM (Σ mountain points), White (best GC rider ≤25). A rider who abandoned a stage is excluded from all subsequent stages and the GC.

Why the GT replaces the knockout cup

Cycling has no elimination bracket; the authentic showpiece is the 3-week GC battle. Treating it as a multi-stage race (rather than a knockout) preserves the "second, marquee competition alongside the league series" role of champman's cup while staying true to the sport.

4.1 Rest Days & Transfers Window

During Grand Tour rest days and the post-Tour August window, a limited mid-season transfer window opens (the real "transfer mercato" period). AI teams and the user may complete a small number of signings; this is throttled separately from the main off-season window.

6. Transfer Subsystem

Source: src/engine/transfers.js (pure functions); glued to state in game.js.

6.1 Valuation

FunctionFormula / rule
askingPrice(team, rider)value × (listed?0.85:1.2) × (key rider?1.3:1), rounded to £25k. Key rider = within 3 of squad best ability.
evaluateBid(team, rider, amount)≥ asking → accepted; ≥ 90% value → countered at midpoint; else rejected.
wageDemand(rider, fromTier, toTier)max(wage×1.1, fairWage); ×1.35 to step down a tier unless offered leadership.
freeAgentWage(rider)fairWage × 1.05.

6.2 Rider Willingness

riderAgrees(rng, rider, fromTier, toTier, offerLeadership): always agrees if toTier ≥ fromTier − 4 (lateral or upward) or if offerLeadership is true. Otherwise reluctant, more so at peak age, capped at 85% rejection. This stops stars dropping to ProTeams without friction — unless they're promised the leader's bib.

6.3 Squad Protection

canRelease(team, rider) refuses if the roster would drop to <22 riders, or if releasing an LDR would leave fewer than 2. This prevents gutting a squad below viability.

6.4 AI Market Behaviour

  • Interest: aiInterest(team, rider) = upgrade over the team's best in that role (+ thin-squad bonus). Weighted pick selects the target.
  • Offers: aiOfferAmount = value × (0.9..1.4).
  • Activity: ~22% weekly chance of an AI bid for a user rider; ~15% weekly chance of an AI-to-AI transfer. Listed riders attract 2.5× interest.

6.5 Scouting & Attribute Masking

Game.attrDisplay(rider, attr) returns exact attributes for the user's own riders and scouted riders; for everyone else it returns a stable range [lo, hi] of half-width 8 that contains the truth but is offset by a per-rider deterministic hash. Sorting uses visibleMid() so the market orders by what the manager can see. A £25k scout report (1 week) flips a rider to "known exactly".

7. Career Loop (game.js)

7.1 advanceWeek(userResult)

The master tick. Steps:

  1. Resolve the calendar event: classic, stage-race stage, or Grand Tour stage.
  2. For each team's entry, #resultFor uses the user's live result if it's the user's race, else simulateRace with a fresh seed from rng.
  3. #applyRaceResult: morale/form/condition/crashes, marketability drift, form guide, season stats, prize money & points → series standings.
  4. If a Grand Tour is live: advanceStage updates GC & jerseys; on the final stage, crown the GT winner + reputation deltas.
  5. #weeklyUpkeep: wages deducted, injuries ticked, condition recovered +16.
  6. #tickInfra & #tickScouts.
  7. #aiTransferActivity & #maybeAiOfferForUserRider.
  8. #updateBoard: confidence from series drift + result; sack if < 15.
  9. week++; monthly awards every 4 weeks; season end if calendar exhausted.

7.4 Board & Job Market

  • Confidence: drift = clamp(expectation − position, −2, +2); result delta +3/0/−4. Clamped [0, 100]. < 15 → sack.
  • Sack: reputation −12; up to 2 job offers from teams with tier ≤ reputation + 15.
  • Poach: after a strong season, 40% chance a team with tier > team.tier + 5 and ≤ reputation + 15 comes calling.
  • Reputation deltas: GT win +20, monument win +10, series champion +8, relegation −8, sack −12.

7.5 Season End

A large deterministic transition. In order: final series standings; prize money; marketability pull; promotion/relegation; season awards (points champion, rider of season, GT winners); board verdict & reputation; sack-or-poach check; rider development & retirements; contract expiries (user must renew deliberately; AI retains 70%); neo-pro intake to ≥22; AI infrastructure; expectation re-ranking by squad strength; calendar & Grand Tour reset; seasonIndex++.

01 · Requirements Specification

Software Requirements Specification (SRS) — functional & non-functional

1. Purpose & Scope

Pelotonman is a single-player cycling-team management career game that runs entirely in the browser. The player assumes the role of a team manager (directeur sportif) at one of 24 real professional teams and is responsible for rider recruitment, race selection, race plans and in-race orders, transfers, sponsorship finances, and meeting the board's expectations across a full season of one-day classics, stage races, and three Grand Tours.

In scope
  • Career mode with a single human-managed team and 23 AI-managed teams.
  • Two 12-team divisions (WorldTour / ProTeam) with promotion/relegation on a 1-season points window and a season-long team points series.
  • Three Grand Tours (Giro d'Italia, Tour de France, Vuelta a España) as marquee multi-stage GC battles, plus a calendar of classics and shorter stage races.
  • Resumable kilometer-by-kilometer race simulation with live team orders from the car.
  • Squad, race plans, transfers, sponsorship/finances, scouting, infrastructure, board, and news systems.
  • Deterministic save/load to localStorage.
Out of scope (deferred)
  • Nationals, world championships, and hotseat multiplayer.
  • Backend services, accounts, cross-device sync.
  • Audio, 3D graphics, or live real-world race data feeds.
  • Women's peloton (a planned roadmap item; the v1 world is the men's WT/ProTeam).

3. Functional Requirements

Requirements are grouped by subsystem. Each carries an ID for traceability into the architecture and test plan.

3.1 Career & World (CAR)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
CAR-01The player shall choose a manager name and one of 24 real teams to begin a career.New game places the chosen team under human control; all others AI.
CAR-02The world shall contain two divisions of 12 real teams with authentic rosters.24 teams; e.g. UAE Team Emirates always contains Tadej Pogačar, Visma | Lease a Bike contains Jonas Vingegaard, Soudal–Quick-Step contains Remco Evenepoel.
CAR-03The season shall be a calendar of real races interleaved across the spring, summer, and autumn.~28 race days: Omloop → Strade Bianche → Milan-San Remo → … → Il Lombardia, including 3 Grand Tours.
CAR-04Two teams shall be promoted and two relegated each season on the season points window.Top 2 of ProTeam swap divisions with bottom 2 of WorldTour (mirroring UCI relegation).
CAR-05Board confidence shall track performance and trigger sacking below a threshold.Confidence < 15 → manager sacked; job market opens.
CAR-06A sacked manager shall be able to take a job at a lesser team.Up to 2 reachable teams offered; accepting continues the career mid-season.
CAR-07Strong seasons shall attract offers from bigger teams.Poach offer raised when reputation exceeds current team tier + 5.
CAR-08Manager reputation shall move with seasons, jersey wins, and sackings.GT win +20, monument win +10, series champion +8, relegation −8, sack −12.

3.2 Race Simulation (RAC)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
RAC-01The race engine shall simulate kilometer-by-kilometer and be pausable at any time.RaceSim.playSegment() advances one segment; pauseRace() halts the timer.
RAC-02The same seed shall produce an identical race.Deep-equal result objects across replays with the same seed.
RAC-03The engine shall model a peloton, breakaway groups, time gaps, climbs, intermediate sprints, and a finish.Result carries groups with gaps; GC/points/KOM classifications computed correctly.
RAC-04Team orders shall be issuable at any pause: chase, sit in / control, send up road, leadout, attack.setOrders() updates per-team directives; affects pace & break composition next segment.
RAC-05The race shall auto-pause at key moments: summit of a climb, breakaway forming, final 5 km, crashes to the user's riders.Key-moment banner shown; user's rider left in trouble until reacting to own crashes.
RAC-06Race plan and aggression shall be changeable mid-race by both user and AI.setRacePlan() remaps roles; AI chases when behind on GC, shuts down when ahead.
RAC-07Stage races shall accumulate GC time across stages; ties broken by stage placings & time trial.GC = Σ stage times + bonuses; white jersey = best ≤25; points & KOM accumulated.
RAC-08Per-rider live ratings out of 10 shall update from race events.Win +1.5, leadout +0.4, pull +0.05/km, dropped −0.6, crash −1.0, etc.
RAC-09Profile-appropriate advantage shall apply: climbers gain on climbs, sprinters on flat finishes, time trialists in ITTs.Climb sector multiplies CLM/LDR power; flat finish multiplies SPR power.
RAC-10One-day classics shall produce a single result; Grand Tour stages shall feed a live jerseys panel.Classic result = {placings, gaps, points}; GT stage result updates GC/points/KOM/white.

3.3 Squad & Riders (SQD)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
SQD-01Each team shall carry a roster of ~26 real riders with role, age, attributes, and hidden traits.~6 LDR, 6 SPR, 8 CLM, 6 ROU per team; consistency & injuryProne hidden.
SQD-02Riders shall have condition, form, morale, wages, value, and contract years.All fields present and updated weekly.
SQD-03The engine shall auto-select the best available 8-rider race team + 4-man reserve for a race plan.selectRaceTeam() deterministic; injured/ill never picked.
SQD-04Out-of-role riders shall incur a penalty.0.8× for a non-leader in LDR slot, 0.75× for severe mismatches.
SQD-05Riders shall develop at season end: youngsters grow, veterans fade, some retire.≤22 grow +4; >32 fade −4; age ≥36 retires at 55% chance.
SQD-06Crashes/mechanicals shall carry real durations; serious crashes force abandonment.injuryWeeks decremented weekly; DNF removes rider from remaining stages of a stage race.
SQD-07A neo-pro intake shall keep rosters viable.Rosters refilled to ≥22 each season end with young real-names talent.

3.4 Race Plans & Tactics (TAC)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
TAC-01Five race plans and three aggressions shall be selectable.Sprint Train, Climbing Block, All-Round, Breakaway, GC Defence; defensive/normal/attacking.
TAC-02The manager shall swap riders between race team, reserve, and rested by clicking.Two clicks complete a swap; lineup persists until invalid.
TAC-03Aggression shall affect breakaway likelihood, chase intensity, and energy expenditure.Attacking ×1.15 break chance / ×1.10 energy burn; defensive ×0.80 / ×0.90.
TAC-04Race plan shall be profile-aware: a sprint plan on an mountain stage warns the user.Mismatch warning shown on pre-race; AI picks profile-appropriate plans.

3.5 Transfers & Scouting (TRF)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
TRF-01The manager shall search the market with role and value filters.searchRiders() returns ranked rows excluding own team.
TRF-02Bids shall be evaluated as accepted / countered / rejected based on asking price.≥ asking → accepted; ≥ 90% value → countered at midpoint; else rejected.
TRF-03Riders shall have willingness that depends on the tier step and project ambition.riderAgrees() reluctant to drop divisions unless offered leadership.
TRF-04Squad-protection rules shall prevent releasing below a floor.≥ 22 riders and ≥ 2 LDR always retained.
TRF-05AI teams shall bid for the user's riders and conduct AI-to-AI transfers.Pending offers arrive in inbox; ~15% weekly AI-to-AI chance.
TRF-06Out-of-contract riders shall be signable on a free.#signFreeAgent path with wage demand only.
TRF-07The manager shall transfer-list and renew contracts.Listed riders cheaper to buy (0.85×) and draw more AI interest.
TRF-08Other teams' attributes shall show as estimate ranges until scouted.£25k scout report (1 week) reveals exact attributes; ranges stable & truth-containing.
TRF-09Pre-race opposition reports shall show form, predicted race team, and danger rider.oppositionReport() returns 8 + form guide + danger rider.

3.6 Sponsorship & Finances (FIN)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
FIN-01Each team shall have a balance, transfer budget, and annual wage bill.Wages deducted weekly; transfer budget ring-fenced from balance.
FIN-02Prize money shall be paid per race result and per jersey, into the team balance.Stage win, jersey income, GC bonus all credited after the race.
FIN-03A marketability score shall grow with wins and drain with poor seasons.×1.010 per win, ×1.002 per top-10, ×0.997 per no-result; floor 200.
FIN-04The title-sponsor deal value shall track marketability and division tier.Annual sponsor income = base(tier) × marketability factor; renews at season end.
FIN-05The manager shall invest in infrastructure (sports science, equipment, performance staff) when funds warrant.Each tier gives a small marginal performance gain; 12-week build, capped at level 5.
FIN-06AI boards shall invest in their own infrastructure when flush.AI invests at balance ≥ 2× cost with reserve maintained.
FIN-07End-of-season prize money shall be paid by series standing and GT jerseys.Series champion of WorldTour receives a major bonus + positional base.

3.7 News & Awards (NWS)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
NWS-01An inbox shall receive time-stamped news items with optional actions.Items capped at 120; offer items carry accept/reject buttons.
NWS-02Monthly Rider of the Month and end-of-season awards shall be issued.RotM requires ≥ 3 race days in the month; season awards include series champion, GT winners, top scorer (points).

3.8 Persistence (SAV)

IDRequirementAcceptance criterion
SAV-01The game shall auto-save to localStorage after every race week.Save written on advanceWeek and after transfers/race plans.
SAV-02A saved game shall restore and continue deterministically.RNG state restored; resumed career replays identically.
SAV-03The save format shall carry a version for compatibility gating.SAVE_VERSION 1; restore throws on mismatch.

4. Non-Functional Requirements

CategoryRequirementTarget / measure
PerformanceEngine-only operations complete without perceptible delay.A full season sim headless completes in < 1 s.
PortabilityNo build step; runs from any static file server.npm run serve (python http.server) suffices.
DeterminismAll randomness flows through one seeded RNG.Identical seed → identical career (tested).
TestabilityEngine is pure & framework-free; UI is a thin client.node:test suite, no DOM dependency in engine.
AccessibilityKeyboard navigability and ARIA labelling on key controls.Team rows focusable + Enter/Space; aria-live on commentary.
FootprintZero runtime dependencies.package.json devDependencies only.
RealismDistributions over thousands of races are believable.~1 breakaway in 3 sticks; climbers win mountain stages; sprinters win flat finishes; time gaps plausible.
Data authenticityReal team, rider, and race names; fictional but plausible attributes.Roster names match real 2024–25 squads; no claim of real ability.

5. Constraints & Assumptions

  • Single-player only. No network multiplayer or shared state.
  • Real names, game-tuned attributes. Names are real; ratings are invented for play balance and do not reflect real-world ability.
  • Browser-local storage. Saves do not leave the device; clearing storage loses the career.
  • ES module browser target. Requires a browser supporting native ES modules (all evergreen browsers).
  • One human team. The other 23 teams are always AI-managed; there is no hotseat mode.
  • Men's peloton v1. Women's WorldTour is a roadmap item, not a v1 constraint.

PELOTONMAN

A retro cycling-team management career game that runs entirely in the browser, modelled on the manager sims of the late 90s but set in the professional road peloton. Take charge of a real WorldTour or ProTeam, manage riders, race plans, transfers, sponsorship, and the board across a full season of classics, stage races, and Grand Tours — with a resumable, pausable race engine you direct from the team car. No build step, no backend; games auto-save to localStorage and replay deterministically from their seed.

Product at a Glance

Elevator pitch

Take the helm of one of 24 real professional teams across the WorldTour and ProTeam divisions and manage riders, race plans, transfers, sponsorship, and the board across a full season of one-day classics, stage races, and three Grand Tours — all in the browser, with a resumable kilometer-by-kilometer race engine you can pause from the team car to issue orders: chase a break, send a domestique up the road, launch the sprinter, or tell your leader to attack on the final climb. No build step, no backend; games auto-save to localStorage and replay deterministically from their seed.

How to read this package

The documents are ordered as a waterfall delivery: requirements flow down into architecture and data model, which flow into engine and UI specifications, which flow into the test plan. Each document is self-contained but cross-references the others.

Specification note. Unlike its football sibling, which was reverse-engineered from an existing codebase, this package is a forward specification: the target design is written here first, and the engine stubs already present (rng.js, riders.js, squad.js) are consistent with it. Real team, rider, and race names are used throughout; rider attributes are game-tuned and do not claim to reflect real-world ability.

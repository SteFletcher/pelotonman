05 · UI / UX Design

Screen flows, design language, and wireframes

1. Design Language

The interface deliberately mimics a retro management terminal: a deep navy surface, dense monospace tables, a points-jersey green accent, and a gold highlight for the maillot jaune / prestige. The aesthetic is the late-90s manager sim — functional, information-dense, and instantly readable — rather than a modern glossy broadcast product.

Design principle

Every screen is a table. The genre lives in dense rows of numbers; the UI leans into that rather than fighting it. The two graphical elements are the race profile chart (an elevation strip on race week) and the peloton track on race day — both present but never loud. Jersey colours reuse the palette tokens, so the maillot jaune, points green, KOM red, and white jersey are always colour-consistent.

3. Screen-by-Screen Specification

Each screen below pairs the design intent with a wireframe. (Real screenshots will be captured from the running app and substituted into this document once the engine is implemented, mirroring the football sibling's screenshot-driven UI doc.)

3.1 Start / New Career

Entry point. A manager-name field, a "Continue saved game" button (shown only when a save exists), and a master team table paired with a live roster inspector. Hovering a team row populates the right-hand roster panel; clicking starts a career. Each team row shows division, unit ratings (LDR/SPR/CLM/ROU), an overall, and the board's expectation. Real team names; the inspector shows real rider names.

3.2 Inbox

The default landing screen after starting or continuing. Time-stamped news items (newest first) with subjects in gold and bodies in body text. Items that carry an offerId render inline Accept / Reject actions — the inbox is not just a log, it's an actionable queue. A badge on the nav button counts pending offers.

3.3 Squad

The roster sorted by role then ability. Columns: role, name (with status tags for INJ/DNF/LISTED), age, power, climb, condition bar, form, morale bar, wage, value, contract years (red when ≤1), and per-rider List / Renew actions. A header summary shows roster size and the annual wage bill.

3.4 Race Plan

Race plan and aggression selectors, an auto-pick button, and three tables: Race Team (8, with role labels — OOR tagged when a rider is out of role), Reserves (4), and Rested. Interaction is click-to-swap: the first click selects a row (gold highlight), the second click on a different row swaps the two riders. A profile-awareness warning appears when the chosen plan mismatches the next race's parcour (e.g. Sprint Train on a mountain stage).

3.5 Calendar & Results

The full season calendar as a table: week number, race name + kind chip (classic / stage / GT), and your team's result (winner + your best placer + placing, or "DNS" if you didn't enter). The current week is highlighted. Grand Tour weeks expand to show the stage number and the day's stage winner + jersey changes. A small elevation strip renders inline for the next race's profile.

3.6 Series Standings

Both divisions side by side, your division first. Standard P/W/PD/RD/PTS columns (races, wins, podiums, top-10s, points). Promotion and relegation zones are marked with coloured left borders (green up, red down); your team row is highlighted. The header notes the movement rule for each division.

3.7 Grand Tours

A three-tab view (Giro / Tour / Vuelta). Each tab shows the stage list with the day's stage winner, the four jersey holders (colour-coded chips), and a live GC top 10 with time gaps. When a GT is won, a gold winner panel replaces the live view with the final podium (GC, points, KOM, white) and your team's stage wins.

3.8 Transfers

The market screen. Role and max-value filters; a results table showing role, name (with status tags), team (or "Free agent"), age, power/climb (exact for own/scouted riders, ranges for everyone else), value, asking price, and per-row Scout / Bid (or Sign for free agents) actions. A header shows transfer budget, balance, and the scout fee. Counter-offers update the asking price in place for a one-click accept.

3.9 Finances

Balance, transfer budget, annual wage bill (flagged red if projected wages exceed budget), and a prize-money panel: season points so far, last race prize, season prize total. The sponsorship panel shows marketability, current title-sponsor deal value, and projected renewal. The infrastructure panel quotes the next build (level + cost + gain) or shows build progress, with the board's refusal reason when funds are insufficient. A top-earners table closes the screen.

3.10 Team & Board

The strategic dashboard. Board confidence and manager reputation as bars; expectation vs current series position; a season top-points-scorers table; and a past-seasons history table (series champions, GT winners, your finish, top scorer). When a bigger team comes calling, a poach-offer panel with Accept / Decline appears at the top.

3.11 Pre-Race

Shown when Continue finds a user race. A three-column layout: your race team (8), your reserves (with the Roll out button), and an opposition report — the scout card with the race profile elevation strip, the opponent's series position, form guide (colour-coded W/T/L), danger rider, and predicted 8. For Grand Tour stages, the current jerseys and GC gaps appear above. This is the payoff for the scouting system: orders now have information to react to.

3.12 Live Race Day

The signature screen. A race header (race name, km done / total, the lead group's gap to the peloton, current sector type), a speed bar (Normal / Fast / Instant + Pause), a commentary feed (auto-scrolling, colour-coded by event type), and a right-hand column with the peloton track and a tabbed rider-stats table.

The track shows the peloton and any breakaway groups as chips positioned by gap, each carrying a live rating (colour-banded cold→hot), the surname, and a team-colour tick. The break's gap is shown as a seconds label between groups. A sector profile mini-chart sits beneath the track, with a marker for the current km. Wins trigger a header flash and a chip pop animation. When the race auto-pauses at a key moment, a gold team-orders panel slides in with the order buttons — Chase, Control / sit in, Send up road, Leadout, Attack — plus mid-race plan/aggression controls and the rider list (on-course + reserves, "X fresh" counter).

3.13 Full Time

Placings (top 20) with time gaps, the winner highlighted in gold, your riders' results flagged, and a symmetrical stats comparison (your team vs the field: avg placing, points taken, KOM points, domestique pulls, crashes). A primary Continue ▸ button commits the result and advances the week. For a GT stage, the jersey changes and GC top-5 deltas appear alongside.

5. Responsive Behaviour

The layout adapts at three breakpoints:

  • > 980px: full three-column pre-race; two-column race layout (commentary + track/stats).
  • ≤ 980px: pre-race collapses to two columns; opposition report spans full width.
  • ≤ 900px: race layout stacks to a single column; track below commentary.
  • ≤ 720px: start-screen select-layout stacks; race header shrinks; commentary height reduces.

6. Accessibility

  • Team-selection rows are focusable (tabindex="0") and activate on Enter/Space.
  • The commentary feed and squad panel use aria-live="polite".
  • All interactive controls have visible :focus-visible outlines in the accent colour.
  • The track carries an aria-label="Peloton track view"; the rider-stats table has a caption.
  • Jersey status is conveyed by colour and a text label (GC/PTS/KOM/WHT), so colour is not the only signal.
  • Race profile charts include an aria-label describing the parcours type and key climbs.
